---

## usecase.1 — enumerate domains

**1. why enumerate all domains?**
→ user needs complete inventory before bulk decisions

**2. why complete inventory?**
→ 300+ domains over years, some forgotten; incomplete view causes missed domains

**3. why does incomplete view matter?**
→ missed domain = failed migration = domain left in squarespace

**4. why is that bad?**
→ split infrastructure, continued dependence on platform we're exiting

**5. why are we exiting?**
→ no api, manual-only management is unsustainable at scale

**root need**: exhaustive visibility before irreversible migration.

---

## usecase.2 — check dns records

**1. why check dns records?**
→ dns configuration must survive the transfer

**2. why must it survive?**
→ dns controls email delivery, website route, verification tokens

**3. why does loss of dns cause harm?**
→ email stops (MX), sites break (A/CNAME), verifications fail (TXT)

**4. why can't users recreate dns?**
→ dns was configured once, often years ago, by someone who left

**5. why is institutional memory lost?**
→ no documentation; dns is "set and forget" infrastructure

**root need**: capture institutional knowledge embedded in dns before it's lost.

---

## usecase.3 — unlock domain for transfer

**1. why unlock domains?**
→ locked domains cannot be transferred (ICANN requirement)

**2. why does ICANN require locks?**
→ domain theft was rampant before clientTransferProhibited became default

**3. why is domain theft bad?**
→ domain is critical infrastructure; theft = business catastrophe

**4. why allow unlock at all?**
→ legitimate transfers require temporary unlock

**5. why is manual unlock per-domain a problem?**
→ 300 manual unlocks = 300 chances for human error or fatigue

**root need**: controlled mass-unlock for legitimate bulk transfer.

**added scenario: awaited verification**

**why add this?** — counterexample review found that "unlocked" doesn't mean "transferable". domain may have awaited verification status that blocks transfer. criterion needed to surface this distinction.

---

## usecase.4 — disable dnssec

**1. why disable dnssec before transfer?**
→ dnssec keys are registrar-specific; mismatch causes resolution failure

**2. why are keys registrar-specific?**
→ private keys must not be shared between organizations

**3. why not just transfer keys?**
→ key rotation is complex; disable-then-re-enable is safer

**4. why is resolution failure severe?**
→ SERVFAIL means domain is effectively dead; no fallback

**5. why does this matter for ~15% of domains?**
→ at 300 domains, ~45 have dnssec; each one could fail silently

**root need**: prevent dns resolution failure from cryptographic mismatch.

---

## usecase.5 — request transfer authorization code

**1. why request transfer codes?**
→ code proves ownership to new registrar (ICANN requirement)

**2. why is proof of ownership needed?**
→ without proof, anyone could claim any domain via transfer

**3. why findsert semantics?**
→ batch retries must not spam user's email with duplicate codes

**4. why is email spam bad?**
→ important code buried in duplicates; user frustration; rate limits

**5. why not just re-request always?**
→ squarespace might rate-limit; user gets confused by multiple codes

**root need**: prove ownership without confusion or spam.

**added scenario: expired code**

**why add this?** — counterexample review found that codes expire (7-14 days). findsert must recognize stale requests and create fresh ones. criterion needed to handle this edge case.

---

## usecase.6 — batch domain transfer workflow

**1. why batch workflow?**
→ 300 domains × 10 minutes = 50+ hours of manual work

**2. why can't squarespace just provide bulk tools?**
→ squarespace doesn't offer api or bulk management (website-builder focus)

**3. why sequential process?**
→ parallel risks rate limits and bot detection

**4. why plan-before-apply?**
→ user must review changes before irreversible operations

**5. why is idempotent resume critical?**
→ 3-hour batch will fail somewhere; restart must be safe

**root need**: transform impossible manual task into automated, recoverable batch.

---

## usecase.7 — authentication

**1. why test authentication?**
→ all operations require authenticated session

**2. why totp specifically?**
→ squarespace supports totp, sms, passkey; only totp is automatable

**3. why reject sms and passkey?**
→ sms requires phone; passkey requires hardware; neither automatable

**4. why auto-refresh sessions?**
→ 3-hour batch outlives session duration

**5. why handle refresh failure?**
→ password change mid-batch must surface clearly, not hang

**root need**: reliable authenticated access for long batch operations.

**added scenario: refresh failure**

**why add this?** — counterexample review found criterion assumed refresh always succeeds. mid-batch credential change or account lock must halt batch with clear error, to enable resume after fix.

---

## usecase.8 — bot detection resilience

**1. why test bot detection resilience?**
→ squarespace may block automation

**2. why might they block?**
→ automated access violates typical TOS

**3. why proceed despite TOS risk?**
→ wisher accepts risk; these are our domains; we're exiting anyway

**4. why test explicitly?**
→ silent block would leave users confused

**5. why control pace?**
→ rate-based detection triggers on rapid sequential requests

**root need**: automation that works reliably despite platform countermeasures.

### usecase.4 — disable dnssec: independent 5-whys

**1. why "dnssec is disabled"?**
→ dnssec keys are registrar-specific

**2. why are keys registrar-specific?**
→ each registrar generates their own DS record; keys can't be shared

**3. why can't keys be shared?**
→ private key security; each registrar manages their own cryptography

**4. why does key mismatch cause failure?**
→ DNSSEC validation fails = SERVFAIL = domain unreachable

**5. why is SERVFAIL worse than no-dnssec?**
→ without dnssec, dns works (no validation). with broken dnssec, dns fails completely.

**root**: prevent complete dns failure from cryptographic mismatch.

**does criterion serve root?** yes — "dnssec is disabled" before transfer prevents mismatch.
