## domain transfer policy (ICANN)

### [FACT] 60-day transfer lock after registration or transfer

> "You cannot transfer a domain name to a different registrar within 60 days of making changes to the registrant name, organization or email address"

> "Additionally, a domain name is within 60 days of initial registration or within 60 days of a previous transfer, which prevents transfers"

[1] [ICANN FAQs for Registrants: Transferring Your Domain Name](https://www.icann.org/resources/pages/name-holder-faqs-2017-10-10-en)

### [FACT] EPP authorization code required for inter-registrar transfers

> "You'll need the AuthInfo code (also called an Authorization Code, AuthInfo code, Auth-Info Code, or transfer code) to make the transfer. This is a unique code created by a registrar to identify the domain name holder and help prevent unauthorized transfers."

> "ICANN regulations mandate that registrars cannot withhold EPP codes from legitimate domain owners"

[1] [ICANN FAQs for Registrants](https://www.icann.org/resources/pages/name-holder-faqs-2017-10-10-en)

### [FACT] registrar must provide auth code within 5 days

> "The registrar is required to provide the Auth-Code to the domain name owner within five calendar days of the owner's request."

[2] [Auth-Code - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Auth-Code)

### [FACT] domain must be unlocked for transfer

> "To transfer a domain, you need to unlock your domain by removing the registrar lock through your current provider's control panel"

[1] [ICANN FAQs for Registrants](https://www.icann.org/resources/pages/name-holder-faqs-2017-10-10-en)

### [SUMP] 60-day lock opt-out may be available

> "At their discretion, some registrars may provide an option for you to opt-out of this 60-day lock period. However, this rule is in place for your protection against unauthorized transfers and the registrar does not have to offer this option."

[1] [ICANN FAQs for Registrants](https://www.icann.org/resources/pages/name-holder-faqs-2017-10-10-en)

## squarespace domain transfer

### [FACT] transfer process takes up to 15 days

> "The transfer process can take up to 15 days to complete."

[5] [Squarespace Help - Domain transfers FAQ](https://support.squarespace.com/hc/en-us/articles/115006554927-Domain-transfers-FAQ)

### [FACT] confirmation email speeds up transfer

> "You'll receive an email with a link to confirm the transfer, and by confirming it as soon as you see the email, you can speed up the domain transfer process—confirming causes your new provider to accept the transfer request and begin the process immediately. If you don't click the link, the transfer process begins in five business days instead."

[5] [Squarespace Help - Domain transfers FAQ](https://support.squarespace.com/hc/en-us/articles/115006554927-Domain-transfers-FAQ)

### [FACT] DNSSEC must be disabled before transfer

> "If you added DNSSEC to your domain, remove it in the advanced settings panel before beginning the transfer, as it's not possible to enable or disable DNSSEC during a pending domain transfer."

[6] [Squarespace Help - Transferring a domain away from Squarespace](https://support.squarespace.com/hc/en-us/articles/205812338-Transferring-a-domain-away-from-Squarespace)

## DNSSEC transfer considerations

### [FACT] DNSSEC causes resolution failures if not disabled

> "To avoid resolution failures, disable DNSSEC before you transfer a domain name. DNSSEC relies on the DS record at the registrar to authenticate DNS responses. If you do not delete the DS record before the transfer, the chain of trust breaks. Recursive resolvers then reject the responses, which causes a service interruption."

[8] [Google Cloud - Migrate or transfer DNSSEC-enabled zones](https://cloud.google.com/dns/docs/dnssec-migrate)

### [FACT] DNSSEC propagation requires 24-48 hours

> "When you disable DNSSEC, it can take 24 to 48 hours for the process to completely finish. This is because of TTL (Time-to-Live) values, which control how long DNS records are cached."

[9] [Cloudflare Community - DNSSEC not turned off before domain transfer](https://community.cloudflare.com/t/dnssec-not-turned-off-before-domain-transfer-cloudflare-showing-invalid-nameserve/776285)

### [OPIN] wait 48 hours after disabling DNSSEC before transfer

> "Wait for 48 hours at least, double-check via Verisign DNSSEC or DIG for the DNSSEC status and DS record(s) existance - make sure there are none and DNSSEC disabled."

[10] [Cloudflare Community - DNSSEC questions about transferring domain](https://community.cloudflare.com/t/dnssec-questions-about-transferring-very-important-domain-from-google-domains-to-cloudflare/453399)

## TOTP 2FA standard

### [FACT] TOTP defined in RFC 6238

> "Time-based one-time password (TOTP) is a computer algorithm that generates a one-time password using the current time as a source of uniqueness, and has been adopted as Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF) standard RFC 6238."

[11] [Wikipedia - Time-based one-time password](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time-based_one-time_password)

### [FACT] default TOTP parameters: 6 digits, 30 seconds

> "Typical TOTP implementation uses 6-digit codes that refresh every 30 seconds."

> "TX (an interval which will be used to calculate the value of the counter, with a default of 30 seconds)"

[12] [RFC 6238](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc6238)

### [FACT] TOTP formula

> "TOTP is defined as TOTP = HOTP(K, T), where T is an integer representing the number of time steps between the initial counter time T0 and the current Unix time, calculated as T = (Current Unix time - T0) / X."

[12] [RFC 6238](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc6238)

### [FACT] SHA1 is standard algorithm

> "RFC 6238 supports HMAC-SHA-1, HMAC-SHA-256, or HMAC-SHA-512."

[12] [RFC 6238](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc6238)
